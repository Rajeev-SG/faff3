# Faff3 Commercial Strategy Memo

Date: 2026-03-22

## Decision

Faff3 should not position itself as a generic AI consultancy.

It should position itself as a specialist partner for turning AI pilots into clear business value inside real workflows, especially for traditional and professional-service businesses that do not have strong in-house AI implementation capability.

## Why this position is attractive

The market signal is strong:

- AI adoption is now broad enough that "AI awareness" is no longer scarce.
- What remains scarce is implementation capability.
- Businesses need help with use-case selection, workflow integration, data quality, privacy, and internal capability.
- Buyers are willing to pay for that help because failed or vague pilots create wasted time, wasted subscriptions, and stalled momentum.

## What `Shaper` use cases mean for faff3

McKinsey's `Shaper` concept is highly relevant.

The highest-value work is not reselling standard AI tools. It is shaping AI into a workflow that is specific to how the client operates.

For faff3, that means:

- use AI where it changes the economics of an existing workflow
- integrate with the client's real tools and processes
- prove value on one use case first
- build reusable delivery assets behind the scenes

## Recommended ideal customer profile

### Primary ICP

Traditional and professional-service businesses with:

- `10-250` employees
- visible admin-heavy workflows
- some AI experimentation already happening
- no strong internal AI implementation lead
- pressure to improve productivity, turnaround time, or customer responsiveness

Examples:

- accounting and finance support firms
- legal-adjacent and compliance-heavy service firms
- property, construction, and contractor operations teams
- agencies and consultancies
- multi-step customer service or quoting businesses

### Secondary ICP

Digital businesses with stalled AI pilots, especially where:

- tools are disconnected
- there is no clear owner
- the pilot works in a demo but not in operations
- nobody can prove ROI yet

## Where faff3 is strongest

Based on the local repo evidence, faff3 is strongest in four areas:

1. `AI delivery operating system`
   Repo setup, working rules, tool orchestration, documentation, and proof.
2. `Workflow integration`
   Connecting models, tools, data, and business steps into a usable flow.
3. `Acceptance and evidence`
   Demonstrating that the result actually works and can be explained.
4. `Operationalisation`
   Taking a useful pilot and making it safe, documented, and repeatable.

## Offer ladder

### 1. AI Workflow Review

Purpose:
- identify the best first use case and the blockers to implementation

Role in the ladder:
- entry offer
- low friction
- fast diagnostic

Price:
- `£3k - £6k`

### 2. First Working AI Use Case

Purpose:
- build one real workflow that saves time, reduces friction, or improves output quality

Role in the ladder:
- main conversion offer
- clearest path from pilot to value

Price:
- `£8k - £18k`

### 3. Make AI Part of the Business

Purpose:
- roll the successful use case into normal operations with documentation, process ownership, and safeguards

Role in the ladder:
- expansion offer
- higher-value implementation

Price:
- `£15k - £35k`

### 4. Ongoing AI Support

Purpose:
- monthly refinement, support, and controlled expansion

Role in the ladder:
- recurring revenue
- client retention

Price:
- `£2.5k - £8k per month`

## Messages to avoid

- "agent enablement"
- "MCP integration"
- "prompt engineering"
- "AI transformation partner"
- "multi-agent workflows"

These phrases are either too technical, too vague, or too abstract for a traditional business owner.

## Messages to use

- make one workflow work
- reduce admin and handoff delays
- connect the tools you already use
- show a clear before-and-after result
- move from experimenting to using AI in the day-to-day business

## Competitive implication

Sunholo's current positioning suggests the overall commercial shape is sound:

- engineering services
- reusable platform/IP
- workshops
- open-source credibility

Faff3 should copy the shape, but not the tone.

Recommended difference:

- Sunholo sounds platform and engineering first.
- Faff3 should sound business-result and workflow first.

## What should be productized versus kept internal

### Productize externally

- workflow review templates
- use-case scoring framework
- proof and demonstration format
- rollout and support pack
- a small number of repeatable workflow patterns

### Keep internal as operating leverage

- exact prompt and harness patterns
- repo workflow internals
- tool wiring conventions
- evaluation and proof tactics
- reusable implementation accelerators

## 90-day go-to-market plan

### Days 1-30

- finalise package names, pricing bands, and offer pages
- produce two short case-style narratives from existing work
- create a simple sales deck and one-page service catalogue
- refine website messaging around "from AI pilots to clear business value"

### Days 31-60

- run outreach and conversations around Package 1 and Package 2 only
- target owner-led service firms and operations-heavy SMEs
- gather objections, language, and buying triggers
- convert the first two delivery wins into structured case studies

### Days 61-90

- tighten the scope of Package 2 using real delivery experience
- package repeatable add-ons such as knowledge base setup or tool consolidation
- launch the monthly support offer for existing clients
- decide whether one vertical deserves a more specific package

## Recommendation

Lead with:

> We help businesses turn AI pilots into one clear, working result in a real workflow, then make that result repeatable.

This is the clearest expression of the value faff3 can actually deliver today.
